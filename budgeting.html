Budgeting:A guide to saving vs spending


Why should I have a budget?


You may be questioning yourself whether you really need a budget and the ultimate answer is YES.
A budgeting plan is an invaluable tool to ensure that live within your means and enable you manage your money.Many people associate it with restrictions and hassle where in fact budgeting can be simple.Stressed? Unexpected bills? Or that dream vacation your best friends are planning? Planning and monitoring your budget will help you identify your wasteful expenses,adapt as quickly as your financial situation changes as well as reward yourself.You may be questioning yourself whether you really need a budget and the ultimate answer is YES.Having this sense of financial clarity is something you NEED throughout life.When you realise the breakdown of your expenses, you may be surprised how things really add up.Budgeting will also help you to spend less than your earn-to scrutinise everything, big and small.Overall, to prioritise what’s important and cutting anything that pushes your spending beyond your income


  • Step 1: Align your budget with your goals and values
  • By defining your goals, it is the first step to help you be motivated whether it is to save money for your future or stop overspending.Make sure your goals are also SMART.

  • Step 2: Distinguish your income and your expenses.
  • The first step is to work out your regular income and based on this, you will give yourself a weekly allowance
  • List your sources of income(including the money that goes into your bank account)such as money you earned from work, from family/friends and work out your expenses such as(including money that goes out your bank account)such as student loans,bills, utilties
    • This works if your a cash user or a credit card user and you can easily check where you have been spending your money.
  • Make a spreadsheet for your regular purchases and list the items that you buy the most/transactions.After you have categorized your expenses,total each category by month.This will give you an idea of your current spending.
  • Make a target to spend an allocated amount of money for each category;debt,education,entertainment,food,events, housing, insurance, utilities, transport, savings, healthcare, appointments and emergencies. A useful tip is to overestimate each allocated budget by 10 percent to give yourself a safety margin.
  • Compile a colour code assorted to each different pay check and then assign a budget in your credit card or amount of cash to spend on within each month. After categorising your spending, you can then put the allocated cash in different spending envelopes or remind yourself that you have a limited allowance for each category.

  • Step 3: Any savings?
  • Set your priorities, and number them by focusing on your needs, wants and other money for debts/savings
  • Carry extra cash with you and leave your debit/credit cards at home
  • Prioritizing bills will allow you to save more money.Set aside a contingency fund in a bank account which delivers the best rate of interest.The amount will vary depending on the idfference between your spending and income.
  • Find out your weakness.From what is left of discretionary spending.select 1-3 areas of spending depending on how much your are able to balance; to spend on your hobbies, luxuries or other things that let you unplug and relax such as camping trips.Figure out what else to cut back!

  • Step 4: Progress, adjust and evaluate?
  • Keep your reciepts organised and review them weekly
  • If you see large numbers indicating higher expenses, you may be shocked so create a reasonable budget for each category such as food,clothing, etc.
  • To prevent yourself from overspending, and to reduce your expenses, limit necessities or find alternatives
  • You can also download an app to help you track your spending monthly and quarterly

Severe debt?

If you’ve passed the date to pay off your missed credit card or loan payments or if you’re behind with so-called ‘priority debts’ such as your: rent, mortgage, fines, bills, tax/credits, do not borrow any money First contact your local free non-profit debt crisis agencies named as Citizens Advice Bureau, StepChange,Debt Charity or National Debtline in order to figure out what steps you should take to make repayments or how to recieve support for your remaining installments.

  1. If you didn’t buy, for example, a car or made any large purchases, but you've used cards or loans to fill gaps, you should be extra cautious.
  2. Do a budget to see where you are wasting cash; you may be leaking money on unused subs or on costly subscriptions.
  3. Work out how much savings you have to cover the debt excluding your necessities and then work out how much longer it will take you to pay off your debt or increase your savings if you give in
  4. Keep a debt diary.Write a note of when you’re likely to over spend.If you can identify trends that lead to overspending you can prevent them easily by replacing them with a solution.Completely avoid even stepping into shopping malls or why not do an activity that is worthwhile like your hobbies?
  5. Get in touch with friends and family to see if they can offer any tips and if you can afford to pay them back.
